Welcome!

Hello! My name is Chenyi Zhao and I'm from Fremont, CA. I'm a 3rd year Computer Science major and I'm doing a math minor.

When I was younger, I used to get yelled at pretty frequently for playing too much video games. And to retaliate, I really thought, "If it's my job, you can't do anything about it." That would be one of the 99 reasons I went into CS.

I'm currently taking CSC 466, CSC 430, and Math 334. There are still so many courses that seem so interesting, so I have no idea what direction I'm going in right now. If you have any recommendations for classes, please let me know!

I won 2nd place at a Girls Nationals and met a World Champion, Gary Kasparov

This was at 2015 PanAm in Colombia, where I won bronze and obtained the title WCM

One thing that really shaped my life was chess. I actually started to play chess when I was about 3 years-old while I was in China. After moving to California, I moved in with my mother and met my step-father for the first time. He asked to play a game with me and he lost within 10 moves. He was so shocked, because he thought he was decent.

I was then taken to the nearest chess club, and from there I was learning chess professionally. I took chess lessons at 7 in the morning daily, I had more chess homework than I had school work, then I traveled to 11 different countries (some I went to multiple times, like Brazil and Peru multiple times) to compete in tournaments. I met so many people and experienced so much pressure I do feel like I matured more than any other kid my age

People do say, "It's just a game," but to me it was more than that. There's so many openings, strategies, and techniques that you can learn. There's midgame theory, endgame theory, and so much more. I have spent an hour on just one move, because it's like a never-ending puzzle with unlimited possibilities.

I genuinely think I wouldn't be majoring in computer science if I didn't play chess.

I didn't miss that shot I swear.
