Welcome!

Hello! My name is Chenyi Zhao and I'm from Fremont, CA. I'm a 3rd-year Computer Science major with a math minor.

When I was younger, I used to get yelled at pretty frequently for playing too much video games. And to retaliate, I thought, "If it's my job, you can't do anything about it." That's one of the 99 reasons I went into CS.

I'm currently taking CSC 466, CSC 430, and Math 334. There are still so many interesting courses, so I have no idea what direction I'm going in yet. If you have any recommendations for classes, please let me know!

I won 2nd place at a Girls Nationals and met a World Champion, Gary Kasparov

This was at 2015 PanAm in Colombia, where I won bronze and obtained the title Woman Candidate Master (WCM).

One thing that really shaped my life was chess. I actually started playing chess when I was about 3 years-old. After moving to California from China when I was 6, I met my step-father for the first time when I moved in with my mother. He asked to play a game with me, and he lost within 10 moves.

After that, I was taken to the nearest chess club, and from there, I began learning chess professionally. I had chess lessons every day at 7 in the morning, more chess homework than schoolwork, and traveled to 12 states and 11 countries (some multiple times, like Brazil and Peru) to compete in tournaments. I met so many people and experienced so much pressure that I feel like I matured faster than other kids my age.

People often say, "It's just a game," but to me, it was more than that. There are so many openings, strategies, and techniques to learn. There's midgame theory, endgame theory, positional theory, and so much more. I've spent an hour on just one move because chess feels like a never-ending puzzle with unlimited possibilities.

I genuinely think I wouldn't be majoring in computer science if I hadn't play chess.

I didn't miss that shot, I swear.
